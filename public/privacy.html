Go Live Simulator Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 12, 2026  ·  Last Updated: May 12, 2026

1. Who We Are

Go Live Simulator is operated by DigiTech Dynamics LLC, based in Minnesota, United States. We are the data controller for the information described in this policy. You can reach us anytime at team@digitechdynamics.com.

2. What Go Live Is (and Isn't)

Go Live is a simulator. There is no real audience, no public broadcast, and no other users watching you. Every viewer, comment, and reaction is generated by artificial intelligence on a private, single-player session. Nothing you do in Go Live is ever published or shared with other Go Live users.

3. Information We Collect

3.1 Information you give us directly

  • Account details — username, age (if you choose to share it), and any preferences you set.
  • Subscription status — handled by Apple and our processor RevenueCat. We do not see your credit-card numbers.

3.2 Information collected automatically

  • Device data — model, iOS version, language, and a non-resettable Firebase Installation ID.
  • Advertising identifier (IDFA) — only if you allow it in the App Tracking Transparency prompt.
  • Crash and performance data — via Firebase Crashlytics and Firebase Performance.
  • Product analytics — events like screen views, button taps, and stream session length via Mixpanel and Firebase Analytics.
  • Attribution data — Branch tracks whether you installed Go Live from a specific ad or link.

3.3 Information processed when you stream

When you start a simulated stream, Go Live sends three things to Google's Gemini API to power the AI audience. This happens approximately every 15 seconds while the stream is active:

  • A short audio clip (10–30 seconds of raw PCM audio) captured from your microphone.
  • A single still camera frame (JPEG, around 768 pixels wide) captured from your camera.
  • A short text summary of recent on-screen comments and transcribed speech.

Audio and camera data are transmitted only while a stream is actively running. When you stop the stream, transmission stops immediately. Go Live does not save the audio or camera frames to your device or to our servers.

5. Google Gemini AI Disclosure

The simulated audience comments in Go Live are generated by Google's Gemini API. During an active stream, short audio clips and single camera frames from your device are sent to Google approximately every 15 seconds so the AI can respond to what is happening.

We use the paid Gemini API tier. Under Google's paid API terms, your data is not used to train Google's AI models. Google may briefly log request data for abuse-detection purposes only. See Google's Gemini API Additional Terms for the full details.

AI-generated audience reactions are core to how Go Live works. You can stop transmission at any time by ending your stream. If you do not want to use AI features, do not start a stream.

6. How We Use Your Information

  • To run the app and generate the AI audience in real time.
  • To process and verify your subscription with Apple and RevenueCat.
  • To understand how the app is used, fix crashes, and improve features.
  • To deliver and measure ads via Google AdMob (only if you allowed tracking).
  • To attribute app installs back to the marketing channel that drove them (Branch).

8. Data We Do Not Collect

  • Health, fitness, or medical data.
  • Precise GPS or background location.
  • Contacts, calendar, or browsing history.
  • Your raw camera or microphone feed for storage (only the real-time samples sent to Gemini as described above).

10. Your Rights and Choices

10.1 Delete your account

In the app, go to Settings → Delete Account. This deletes your Firebase account, RevenueCat customer record, Mixpanel profile, and all on-device data within 30 days. You can also email team@digitechdynamics.com to request deletion.

10.2 Manage your subscription

Open Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions on your device.

10.3 Opt out of ad tracking

Decline the App Tracking Transparency prompt, or change it later in Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking.

10.4 GDPR rights (EEA, UK, Switzerland)

You can request access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, or object to processing. Email team@digitechdynamics.com. We rely on legitimate interest for analytics, contractual necessity for the subscription, and consent for advertising-related processing.

10.5 CCPA / CPRA rights (California)

You have the right to know what we collect, to delete it, to correct it, to opt out of any sale or sharing of personal information, and not to be discriminated against for exercising your rights. We do not sell personal information. Some advertising activity may qualify as "sharing" under CPRA; you can opt out by declining the ATT prompt or by emailing us.

11. Data Retention

Account data and analytics are retained while your account is active. After you delete your account (or request deletion by email), we delete your account record and analytics profile within 30 days, subject to short backup-retention periods that auto-expire. Audio and camera data sent to Gemini during streams is processed in real time and is not retained by us beyond the request.

12. Security

All network traffic is encrypted using TLS. Secrets and authentication tokens are stored in the iOS Keychain. We do not store passwords; sign-in is handled through Firebase Authentication.

13. International Transfers

Our processors (Firebase, RevenueCat, Mixpanel, Branch, Google AdMob, Google Gemini API) operate primarily in the United States. If you use Go Live from outside the United States, your information will be transferred to and processed in the United States and potentially other countries where these providers operate.

14. Children's Privacy

Go Live is rated 4+ on the App Store. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or under 16 in the EEA/UK). If a parent or guardian believes a child has provided personal information in Go Live, please email team@digitechdynamics.com and we will delete it. For more on children's privacy, see the U.S. Federal Trade Commission's COPPA guidance.

15. Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date above and, where appropriate, notify you in the app before the change takes effect.
